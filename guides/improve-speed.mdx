## Understanding Typing Speed

Typing speed is measured in **Words Per Minute (WPM)**, which represents how many words you can accurately type in 60 seconds. In WPM Typing Tutor, a "word" is standardized to 5 characters, so typing 250 characters correctly in one minute equals 50 WPM.

</Note>

## The Speed vs. Accuracy Balance

Many beginners make the mistake of prioritizing speed over accuracy. However, in WPM Typing Tutor (and real-world typing), **accuracy is more important than raw speed** because:

- Each error reduces your final WPM score significantly
- Correcting mistakes takes more time than typing correctly the first time
- Bad habits from inaccurate typing are hard to break

<Warning>
  **Always maintain 95% accuracy or higher.** If your accuracy drops below 95%, slow down and focus on typing correctly. Speed will naturally increase as your muscle memory improves.
</Warning>

### The 95% Rule

Follow this principle for optimal improvement:

```
If accuracy ≥ 95%  → You can try to increase speed
If accuracy < 95%  → Slow down and focus on accuracy
```

## Fundamental Techniques

Master these fundamental techniques to build a strong foundation:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Master Proper Positioning First" icon="keyboard">
    Before focusing on speed, ensure you have proper keyboard positioning:
    
    - Index fingers on **F** and **J** (marked with dots)
    - All fingers resting on the home row
    - Correct finger assignments for each key
    - Wrists elevated, not resting on the desk
    
    See our [Keyboard Positioning Guide](/guides/keyboard-positioning) for detailed instructions.
    
    <Tip>
      Proper positioning is **non-negotiable** for achieving high speeds. You cannot reach 100+ WPM with poor finger positioning.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Never Look at the Keyboard" icon="eye-slash">
    Touch typing means typing without looking at your hands:
    
    - Train yourself to keep your eyes on the screen at all times
    - Use the tactile markers on **F** and **J** to maintain position
    - If you must look, only glance briefly to reorient, then return eyes to screen
    - Consider covering your hands with a cloth during practice sessions
    
    **Why this matters:** Looking at the keyboard adds 0.2-0.5 seconds per glance, which can reduce your WPM by 20-30 points.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Develop a Consistent Rhythm" icon="music">
    Good typists type in a smooth, rhythmic pattern:
    
    - Avoid "burst" typing (fast then slow then fast)
    - Maintain steady, consistent keystrokes
    - Think of typing like playing a piano — smooth and rhythmic
    - Practice typing to music with a steady beat to develop rhythm
    
    Rhythm reduces mental fatigue and increases sustained speed over longer sessions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use All Your Fingers" icon="hand">
    Many self-taught typists only use 2-4 fingers:
    
    - This creates a hard ceiling on your maximum speed (usually 40-50 WPM)
    - Proper 10-finger typing is required to reach 70+ WPM
    - Even if it feels slower at first, commit to using correct fingers
    - The temporary speed decrease is worth the long-term gains
    
    **Bottom line:** If you want to type fast, you must use all 10 fingers with proper assignments.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Progressive Training Method

Follow this structured approach to systematically improve your speed:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Establish Your Baseline">
    First, determine your current speed and accuracy:
    
    - Play WPM Typing Tutor on **Easy** level 3 times
    - Record your WPM and accuracy for each attempt
    - Calculate your average — this is your baseline
    
    Example baseline: 45 WPM at 96% accuracy
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Realistic Goals">
    Create achievable milestones:
    
    - **Short-term (1-2 weeks):** Increase by 5 WPM while maintaining 95%+ accuracy
    - **Medium-term (1-2 months):** Increase by 10-15 WPM
    - **Long-term (3-6 months):** Double your baseline speed
    
    Example progression: 45 → 50 → 60 → 75 → 90 WPM
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Practice with Accuracy Focus">
    For 70% of your practice time:
    
    - Type at a comfortable speed where you maintain 98-100% accuracy
    - Focus on proper technique and finger positioning
    - Build muscle memory through consistent, correct repetition
    - Use **Easy** and **Medium** levels
    
    This builds the foundation for higher speeds.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Practice with Speed Focus">
    For 30% of your practice time:
    
    - Push yourself to type faster than comfortable
    - Accept that accuracy will temporarily drop to 90-95%
    - Challenge yourself with **Hard** level
    - Push boundaries while maintaining the 95% minimum
    
    This trains your fingers to move faster while maintaining acceptable accuracy.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track Progress Weekly">
    Every week, test yourself:
    
    - Take 3 attempts at your target difficulty level
    - Record your best WPM and average accuracy
    - Compare to previous week's results
    - Adjust your training focus based on results
    
    If your accuracy is above 97%, you can push speed harder. If accuracy drops below 95%, spend more time on accuracy-focused practice.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Advanced Techniques

Once you've mastered the fundamentals (50+ WPM at 95%+ accuracy), incorporate these advanced techniques:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Learn Common Bigrams and Trigrams" icon="spell-check">
    Bigrams (2-letter combinations) and trigrams (3-letter combinations) are the building blocks of words:
    
    **Common bigrams:** th, he, in, er, an, re, on, at, en, nd, ed, es, or, te, of
    
    **Common trigrams:** the, and, ing, ion, tio, ent, for, her, tha, ere
    
    Practice these combinations specifically:
    ```
    the the the and and and ing ing ing
    tion tion tion ent ent ent
    ```
    
    Your brain will start recognizing these as single units rather than individual letters, significantly increasing speed.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Anticipate the Next Word" icon="brain">
    Advanced typists start thinking about the next word while typing the current one:
    
    - Read 2-3 words ahead of what you're typing
    - Your fingers type the current word while your brain prepares for the next
    - This creates a "pipeline" effect that increases overall speed
    - Requires practice but can add 10-20 WPM once mastered
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Minimize Hand Movement" icon="arrows-minimize">
    Efficiency is key to high speeds:
    
    - Keep your fingers close to the home row at all times
    - Use minimal finger extension to reach other keys
    - Return to home position immediately after each keystroke
    - Avoid unnecessary wrist or hand movement
    
    Watch your hands while typing slowly — eliminate any wasted motion.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Practice Your Weak Keys" icon="dumbbell">
    Everyone has specific keys that slow them down:
    
    - Identify which keys cause you to hesitate or make errors
    - Create targeted practice sessions for those specific keys
    - Common problem keys: Q, Z, X, P, and punctuation marks
    - Practice words containing your weak keys repeatedly
    
    Example weak key practice for 'X':
    ```
    extra exit exam example text next six
    ```
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Type in Bursts for Long Sessions" icon="bolt">
    For extended typing sessions:
    
    - Type in focused 20-minute intervals
    - Take 2-3 minute breaks between sessions
    - During breaks, stretch your hands and wrists
    - This prevents fatigue and maintains high accuracy
    
    Studies show that shorter, focused sessions are more effective than long, unfocused ones.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Effective Practice Routine

Consistency is more important than duration. Follow this daily 20-minute routine:

### Daily 20-Minute Practice Plan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Warm Up (3 minutes)">
    Start with slow, accurate typing:
    
    - Type the home row keys: `asdf jklñ asdf jklñ`
    - Type common words slowly: `the and for are but not you all can`
    - Focus on proper positioning and rhythm
    - Goal: 100% accuracy, no speed pressure
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Accuracy Practice (7 minutes)">
    Type at comfortable speed with maximum accuracy:
    
    - Use WPM Typing Tutor **Easy** or **Medium** level
    - Aim for 98-100% accuracy
    - Focus on smooth, consistent rhythm
    - If you make errors, slow down slightly
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Speed Push (5 minutes)">
    Push beyond your comfort zone:
    
    - Use WPM Typing Tutor **Medium** or **Hard** level
    - Type 10-15% faster than your average speed
    - Accept 90-95% accuracy during this session
    - Focus on maintaining rhythm despite the faster pace
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Weak Point Practice (3 minutes)">
    Target your specific weaknesses:
    
    - Practice words containing your problem keys
    - Focus on common bigrams/trigrams you struggle with
    - Type slowly and accurately to build correct muscle memory
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Cool Down Test (2 minutes)">
    End with a measured attempt:
    
    - Take one serious attempt at your target difficulty level
    - Record your WPM and accuracy
    - Compare to your baseline and previous sessions
    - Celebrate improvements, no matter how small!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Consistency is key!** Practicing 20 minutes daily is far more effective than practicing 2 hours once per week. Daily practice builds muscle memory faster.
</Tip>

## Common Speed Plateaus

Most typists hit these common plateaus. Here's how to break through:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The 40 WPM Plateau" icon="1">
    **Cause:** Usually due to improper finger positioning or looking at the keyboard
    
    **Solution:**
    - Commit to proper 10-finger positioning
    - Stop looking at the keyboard completely
    - Temporarily slow down to rebuild correct technique
    - Use the [Keyboard Positioning Guide](/guides/keyboard-positioning)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="The 60 WPM Plateau" icon="2">
    **Cause:** Inconsistent rhythm, lack of anticipation, or weak keys
    
    **Solution:**
    - Practice common bigrams and trigrams
    - Work on reading ahead while typing
    - Identify and practice your 5 weakest keys
    - Focus on maintaining consistent rhythm
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="The 80 WPM Plateau" icon="3">
    **Cause:** Excessive hand/finger movement or mental fatigue
    
    **Solution:**
    - Minimize finger extension and hand movement
    - Practice shorter, more focused sessions
    - Work on efficiency of motion
    - Consider taking a 1-week break to prevent burnout
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Level-Specific Strategies

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Easy Level">
    **Best for:** Beginners (0-40 WPM) or learning new techniques
    
    **Strategy:**
    - Focus entirely on accuracy (aim for 98-100%)
    - Use this level to practice proper positioning
    - Don't worry about speed at all
    - Perfect level for warming up
    
    **Goal:** Consistently hit 95%+ accuracy before moving to Medium
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Medium Level">
    **Best for:** Intermediate typists (40-70 WPM) building speed
    
    **Strategy:**
    - Balance speed and accuracy (aim for 95-97%)
    - Push slightly beyond your comfort zone
    - Practice common word patterns
    - Most versatile level for daily practice
    
    **Goal:** Reach 60+ WPM at 95%+ accuracy before attempting Hard
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Hard Level">
    **Best for:** Advanced typists (70+ WPM) or speed challenges
    
    **Strategy:**
    - Push maximum speed while maintaining 95% minimum accuracy
    - Use for 30% of practice time (mostly use Medium)
    - Great for competing on the leaderboard
    - Expect higher error rates — this is normal
    
    **Goal:** Break 100+ WPM while maintaining 95%+ accuracy
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Quick Tips for Immediate Improvement

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tip: Adjust Your Seating" icon="chair">
    - Sit at the proper height so your elbows are at 90 degrees
    - Keep feet flat on the floor
    - Position monitor at eye level
    - Sit one forearm's length from the keyboard
    
    Proper ergonomics can immediately improve comfort and speed.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Tip: Type With Your Ears" icon="ear-listen">
    - Good typing has a consistent, rhythmic sound
    - Listen to the pattern of your keystrokes
    - Irregular sounds indicate irregular rhythm
    - Aim for a steady "tap-tap-tap" sound
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Tip: Use the Space Bar Strategically" icon="space-bar">
    - Hit space with your thumb immediately after the last letter
    - Don't pause before pressing space
    - Consistent spacing prevents rhythm disruption
    - Practice: `word space word space word space`
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Tip: Breathe While You Type" icon="wind">
    - Many people hold their breath while concentrating
    - This causes tension and reduces sustained performance
    - Breathe naturally and rhythmically
    - Take a deep breath before starting each level
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
